The Scene Palette

Once you have added or created a component in your scene, you will notice that it not only appears directly on the scene but also as an item in the Scene Palette. This palette entry makes it easy to locate your component anywhere in the scene. The Scene Palette also lets you manage your scene, organize components into categories, and perform bulk actions.

A visual view of the Scene Palette

1 Minimizer Hides the Scene Palette, allowing a cleaner interface and more room on your scene. Click the Scene tab (#12) to re-expand it.
2 Scene Settings Opens the scene settings menu.
3 Quick Actions Provides quick links to export the scene, save the scene, and batch export components.
4 Scene Name Displays the current scene name. This is a text input field where you can rename your scene.
5 Category Indicator Displays the component list for the currently selected category.
6 Scene Component List A list of all components in the currently selected category.
7 Scene Category Tabs Tabs that let you switch between categories in the scene.
8 Category Buttons Quick links for adding components from different sources, such as local files, the asset stash, or external libraries.
9 Create Category Button Creates a new scene category. Any selected items will immediately be transferred to this category, and the component list (#6) will update to display it.
10 Component List Item Clicking on a component entry opens its properties for editing.
11 Add New Scene Button Adds a new blank scene or loads an existing one.
12 Scene Selector Tabs Use these tabs to switch between your scenes.
13 Move Selected to Category Moves any selected components on the canvas to the currently open category in the Scene Palette.
14 Category-wide Actions Actions that affect all components in the current category, such as selecting all, locking/unlocking, or removing them from the scene canvas.

Useful Features of the Scene Palette

  • Clicking on a component in the list will also select it on the scene.
